“Music is the way our memory sing to us across time.”

- Lance Morrow

During our research and interviews with the
elderly, we collected several stories of how they
encouter the music that accompany them
through the years.
Felicita , 89 y/o
“

I like listening to music especially because it keeps me company
during the day, I often feel alone and while I wait for my
grandchildren who come to visit me, I usually turn on the TV on
the channel that broadcasts music, but often I find only modern
foreigners songs that I don't understand. I like to listen to quiet music,
but above all the opera music and the songs of my youth; it reminds
me of the good old time when I used to go in opera theatre to see
ballets and musicals.

”

Nessun Dorma
50s, Relaxing
Antonia , 81 y/o
“

I normally listening to music when I am watching the television and I
prefer listening to it in the morning when I wake up or at night before I
go to sleep. I love listen to music and dance because I feel really free
when I’m dancing. Unfortunately, aside the television the only other
way I can listening to music is asking my granddaughter to put it on
her cellphone, but she is not always home and we normally fight
because she want to her songs and I want to her my songs, but as I
don’t have other way of listening to music I end up listening to the
songs I am not so interested and it is a little bit frustrating

”

Escrito nas Estrelas
70s, Happy
Xiufen Zhu, 60 y/o
“

The first song that comes to my mind is this beautiful melody, a
movie’s theme song when I was a kid. The film is about old-time
teenagers’ lives, and some of its most important scenes are shoot at
Beihai Park, which is only a few minutes’ walks from my home back
then. You would probably think this is a bit stupid because the song is
a children’s ballad now, but the movie scenes of that lively and
passionate teenagers’ days are just the same as what I saw and what
I lived, which makes this song so dear and touching to me.

”
