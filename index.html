“How might we help the elderly enjoy
old music that they can relate to?”
The
Context

Nowadays, the elderly are increasingly forced to interface themselves with new technologies: these technologies are very distant from their way of thinking and interacting with the world; meanwhile, they are more used to interface themselves with physical objects and tangible concepts.

If only they had a chance to understand and use them easily, these technologies could improve the elderly’s daily activities helping them in facing difficulties and relieving the sense of loneliness which often pervades their days making them feel distant and isolated from the world.

The
Problem

To relieve the sense of loneliness, the elderly often turn on the television or the radio, using sounds as a background that keeps them company during the day. Usually, the music does not match their taste, and they have trouble choosing freely what to listen to because of the technological gap.

The Big Idea

It is from here that we want to give a meaning to
that “background sound”:

We want to provide a way to engage elderly
through the music, which is seen as an opportunity
to make the elderly recall their memories, and
consequently express themself, collecting and
sharing their own experience with it.

Conclusions
and Design
Reccomendations

Tune is born to give the elderly the opportunity to recover music of their time, collect their special songs related to their own expeirences and make them able to share and express themselves through it. To enable the elderly in doing this, we designed a system to make music tangible, taking the advantages of the NFC technology embedded within cards that can save the song played by a device specially designed for elderly too. This device, that plays mostly songs before the 2000s, has an old radio style appearance with analogic controllers studied to be easily recognizable and accpeted by elderly as a product related to music.
